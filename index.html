A Proposal for Bright Prospect · Summer Academy 2026

Every student in this program has a story worth telling. This week, we capture it — for the donors who made it possible, the community that surrounds it, and the students who lived it.

Working With Me

How a Lenz Fotos engagement runs, start to finish — eight steps from first conversation to final debrief. Payment milestones are marked in gold.

Step 01

Discovery conversation

We sit down — in person or virtually — and I listen. You tell me who the students are, what the week looks like, and who needs to see this content. No pitch, no package pressure.

What I need from you: the week's schedule, names of any guest speakers, and what a successful deliverable looks like to Debra specifically.

30–45 min · Before booking
Step 02

Proposal + agreement

I send a written proposal within 48 hours — scope, deliverables, timeline, and price all itemized. We agree on everything in writing before a single photo is taken.

You'll receive: itemized deliverable list, shoot dates confirmed, payment schedule, and cancellation policy.

Reference

Interview Questions

The prompts I bring to camera — written to draw out the story, not the soundbite.

  • Where were you a year ago, and where do you see yourself now?
  • What did you think college was like before Bright Prospect, and what do you think now?
  • Who in your life believed in you before you believed in yourself?
  • What does graduating from college mean for your family?
  • If you could send a message to your future self on graduation day, what would you say?
  • Is there someone out there who made this possible for you — what would you want to say to them?
  • What does it mean to know that people who don't even know you believed in you enough to invest in this?
  • What's going through your mind right now, being here?
  • What do you want to remember about today?
  • Is this somewhere you ever imagined yourself as a kid?
  • What do you wish someone had told you at their age?
  • Was there a moment where you almost didn't make it — and what changed?
  • What do you see in this generation that gives you hope?
  • If one student in this room remembers one thing you said five years from now, what do you want it to be?

Let's tell this year's story together, Debra.
